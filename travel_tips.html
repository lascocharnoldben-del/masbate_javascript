Travel Tips

Important safety and financial reminders for a hassle-free stay.

Always check marine weather forecasts before booking island-hopping tours. Always wear provided life jackets on outrigger boats traveling to Buntod Sandbar or Ticao Island.

Bicol cuisine is famous for its heat. Sample small portions first if you are sensitive to spicy ingredients like siling labuyo.

Most small shops and local drivers only accept cash. It is best to withdraw money at an ATM in the city center before exploring.

Keep your devices fully charged during long transit times or outdoor adventures where electricity grid sockets aren't accessible.

Cellular signals can be weak or non-existent in remote regions. Pre-download maps on your phone for reliable offline navigation.

Travel Guide

How to get around and what to expect during your visit to Masbate.

1. Best Time to Visit

Plan your trip from March to May for calm island waters. Visit around April to witness the exciting Rodeo Masbateño Festival.

2. Transportation

Use local tricycles within Masbate City. For travel between municipalities, passenger vans and jeepneys are readily available.

3. Arrival Points

Fly straight into Moises R. Espinosa Airport in Masbate City, or ride a ferry/RORO from Pilar or Sorsogon directly to the port.

4. What to Pack

Pack swimwear, reef-safe sunscreen for the sandbars, and comfortable boots or closed shoes if you plan to visit the rolling ranches.

5. Local Etiquette

Practice "Leave No Trace" rules at eco-tourism spots. Always request permission before exploring private ranch fields or farmland boundaries.
